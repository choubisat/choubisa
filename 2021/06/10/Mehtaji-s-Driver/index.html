The Dilema of driver

“Even the moon is , aagey se right behenji”, Complained Shalini. A strange place brings out many frustations from oneself. Simple daily things become horrendously big tasks. Navigating to shops, markets, parlor and salons for routine needs bores a challenge. Like a body rejecting the environment outside, it creates an unspitting vomitting sensation in the stomach. Shalini has caught up with a similar bout of feelings. Vimal understood it and noded helplessly towards his wife. A wry smile, that attempted to balance exaggerated pouring from Shalini.

Shalini and Vimal have shifted to Delhi from Jaipur. By calendar, six months have gone by, though they had remained locked in their apartment for better part of it. They did not get much opportunity to start a new life here. Gone were the simple namastes with neighbours or the random tea invitations, neither any ganging up on the nights to catch something new on TV. In normal times, it was supposed to be exciting phase of exploring new vistas and find a comfortable new social life at Delhi. However, its was replaced with mostly remaining caged between unfamiliar walls.
